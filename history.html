The paragraph:

  1. Founding and Myth

    2. Rome was founded on April 21, 753 BC, according to legend, by twins Romulus and Remus, sons of the god Mars, who were raised by a she-wolf on the Palatine Hill.

  2. Transition from Kingdom to Republic

    3. In 509 BC, Romans overthrew their last king, Tarquin the Proud, and established the Roman Republic, where power was shared between the Senate and elected consuls.

  3. Punic Wars

    4. Rome fought three wars against Carthage, the most famous being the Battle of Cannae (216 BC), where Rome suffered massive losses.

  4. Transition to Empire

    5. In 27 BC, Augustus became the first Roman Emperor, starting the Pax Romana, a period of relative peace that lasted nearly 200 years.

  5. Division and Fall

    6. In 395 AD, the Roman Empire split into Eastern and Western halves.

    The Western Roman Empire fell in 476 AD due to barbarian invasions, while the Eastern Empire (Byzantine Empire) lasted until 1453 AD.
